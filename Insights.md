# 🔍 Insights — Logistics Fleet Performance Analysis

---

## 💰 Revenue & Profitability

- Total **Net Revenue reached $484.53M** across the full period (2018–2019), with a remarkably high **profit margin of 99.22%** ($480.76M profit), indicating very low cost relative to revenue scale.
- Revenue shows a **clear upward trend** from early 2018 through mid-2019, peaking at **$36M in a single month**, before slightly declining toward Q3 2019.
- **West Virginia (WV)** leads all states in net revenue at **$65M**, followed by Oklahoma ($52M) and South Carolina ($40M), while states like Illinois, Delaware, and South Dakota each contribute only $10M.

---

## 🚛 Truck Type Performance

- The fleet consists of **23 trucks** handling **5,032 total freights** over 731,574 miles.
- **TRAILER trucks dominate** the fleet with 14 units, generating **$261.75M in revenue** and a profit of **$259.88M (99.29% margin)** — the highest performing category.
- **SEMI-TRAILER** trucks (3 units) achieved a **99.24% profit margin** on $90.92M revenue, showing strong efficiency despite fewer units.
- **BOX trucks** (5 units) generated $88.66M revenue with a **99.13% margin**, while **TRACTOR** (1 unit) contributed $42.5M at a **98.97% margin** — the lowest but still highly profitable.

---

## 🏷️ Trailer Type Performance

- **Reefer trailers** are the top performer by profit at **$279.26M**, significantly ahead of Fridge ($159.44M) and Dry ($42.06M).
- **Dry trailers** account for only **8.7% of total profit**, suggesting either lower utilization or lower-value freight assignments.
- In terms of cost, **Reefer trailers dominate at 62.32%** of cost by trailer type, while **Fridge accounts for 26.05%** and **Dry only 11.63%** — costs are aligned with revenue contribution.

---

## 💸 Cost Structure

- Total operational cost is **$3.77M**, broken down into:
  - **Fixed Costs: 69%** ($2.6M) — the dominant cost driver
  - **Fuel Cost: 23.91%** (~$901.89K)
  - **Maintenance Cost: 7.09%** (~$267.41K)
- **Cost Per Ton: $5.68** and **Cost Per Mile: $5.16**, both indicating lean operations.
- Fixed and variable costs show **seasonal spikes**, particularly around mid-2018 and early 2019, possibly linked to increased freight demand or maintenance cycles.
- **TRAILER trucks** incur the highest total cost at **$1.87M**, consistent with their dominant fleet size and freight volume.

---

## 👨‍✈️ Driver Performance

- The **top 5 drivers by cost** are: Amman Vega ($393K), Unknown ($384K), Ridwan Greaves ($375K), Maheen Nicholls ($310K), and Stevie Schofield ($280K).
- The appearance of **"Unknown"** as the second highest cost entry suggests possible **data quality issues** or unassigned freight records where driver information was not captured.
- Cost concentration among the top 5 drivers represents a significant portion of total variable costs.

---

## 📦 Freight Volume Trends

- Total freights reached **5K shipments**, with volume trending upward from **714 in Q1 2018** to a peak of **918 in Q2 2019**, before dropping to **669 in Q3 2019**.
- The **Q2 2019 peak** aligns with the revenue peak, confirming that freight volume is a strong driver of revenue performance.
- The **Q3 2019 decline** in both freights and revenue may indicate seasonality, reduced demand, or operational constraints.

---

## 🗺️ Geographic Distribution

- Revenue is **heavily concentrated** in a handful of states — the top 3 states (WV, OK, SC) account for a disproportionate share of total revenue.
- Several states contribute only **$10M each** (DE, IL, SD), pointing to underdeveloped markets or limited route coverage.
- The **Midwest and Southern states** (MO, KY, AZ) show moderate performance, suggesting room for growth.
